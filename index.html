Are you dreaming of owning your dream home while also maximizing your savings? We have fantastic news for you! Taking a home loan not only helps you achieve the home of your dreams but also comes with incredible tax benefits that can make a significant impact on your financial well-being.
 

under section 80c

By opting for a joint home loan, each co-applicant can claim the principal component of the EMI under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. A maximum amount of Rs 1.5 Lakh per financial year is allowed.

UNDER SECTION 24(B)

Under Section 24(B) of the Income Tax Act, both co-applicants can claim the interest component of the EMI. The tax benefit you can claim is capped at Rs 2 Lakh per financial year for the interest payments.

under section 80ee

As first-time home buyers, you can enjoy additional home loan tax benefits under Section 80EE of the Income Tax Act. You can claim a maximum amount of Rs 50,000 on the interest payment per financial year.

BETTER INTEREST RATES FOR A WOMAN CO-APPLICANT

You can avail better interest rates if the joint home loan is taken with a woman co-applicant. Hence, if you are applying for a loan with your spouse, you can get a concessional rate that is lower than the standard rate.

Increased loan eligibility

Your home loan eligibility is decided based on your credit score, repayment history, income, and other such factors. In case you have a lower income or credit score, adding a co-applicant with a better track record can help enhance your loan eligibility.
